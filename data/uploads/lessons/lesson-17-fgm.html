⚖️ What is FGM?

Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) refers to procedures that intentionally alter or cause injury to female genital organs for non-medical reasons. It is internationally recognised as a violation of human rights and is illegal in Kenya under the Prohibition of FGM Act 2011.

TypeDescription
Type 1 (Clitoridectomy)Partial or total removal of the clitoris
Type 2 (Excision)Removal of clitoris and labia minora
Type 3 (Infibulation)Narrowing of vaginal opening by cutting and sealing labia — most severe
Type 4 (Other)Pricking, piercing, scraping, or burning genital area
⚠️ In Kenya: FGM is most prevalent among Maasai, Somali, Kalenjin, and some Kisii communities. Over 21% of Kenyan women have undergone FGM. Anyone who performs, assists, or fails to report FGM faces up to 3 years imprisonment or a fine of KES 200,000.
🔍 Why Does FGM Happen? The Causes
  • Cultural tradition: Seen as a rite of passage into womanhood
  • Social pressure: Families fear social exclusion if they don't comply
  • Marriageability: Belief that uncut girls are undesirable as wives
  • Myth about sexuality: False belief that it controls female sexual desire
  • Misrepresented religion: FGM is not prescribed by Islam, Christianity, or any major religion
