💪 What Are Life Skills?

Life skills are the abilities that help you navigate daily challenges, make good decisions, build relationships, and achieve your goals. The World Health Organization identifies 10 core life skills every young person needs.

Life SkillWhy It Matters in Kenya
CommunicationExpress yourself clearly; avoid misunderstandings
Decision-MakingChoose wisely when facing pressure, relationships, career
Critical ThinkingEvaluate information — important in the age of fake news
Problem-SolvingFind solutions without panicking or giving up
Self-AwarenessUnderstand your strengths, weaknesses, values
EmpathyUnderstand others' feelings — builds respect and cooperation
Stress ManagementHandle exam pressure, family stress, peer pressure
Time ManagementBalance school, chores, social life effectively
AssertivenessSay NO to drugs, sex, or wrong choices confidently
ResilienceRecover from failure, disappointment, or setbacks
🗣️ Communication Skills in Practice

Effective communication is both speaking AND listening. Many conflicts arise not from disagreement but from poor communication.

TypeKey SkillExample
VerbalClear, calm, specific language"I feel hurt when you ignore me" vs "You always ignore me!"
Non-verbalOpen body language, eye contactNodding, facing the speaker, uncrossed arms
ListeningActive listening — full attentionPhone away, no interrupting, reflecting back
WrittenClear, respectful toneProfessional texts and emails
🔑 "I" Statements: Say "I feel..." instead of "You always..." — this prevents the other person from becoming defensive and keeps conversation productive.
🇰🇪 Classroom Scenario: Zawadi's group ignored her project ideas. Instead of sulking or attacking ("You never listen to me!"), she said: "I feel frustrated when my ideas aren't considered. Can we give everyone 2 minutes to share?" The group responded positively. One assertive, clear statement changed everything.
🧭 Decision-Making in Real Life

Good decisions don't happen by accident. They follow a clear process:

1. Define the problem → 2. Consider all options → 3. Evaluate pros and cons → 4. Choose & act → 5. Reflect on the outcome

🇰🇪 Kipchoge's Decision: Was offered money by an older student to carry a package without being told what was inside. He used the decision-making model: What are my options? What are the risks? Could this involve drugs? He refused. Later found out the package contained cannabis — he would have been arrested.
