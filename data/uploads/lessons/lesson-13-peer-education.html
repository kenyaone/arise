🎓 What is Peer Education?

Peer education is when people from similar backgrounds — same age, school, or community — educate each other. Research shows that young people are more likely to listen to and trust peers than adults on sensitive topics like SRH, drugs, and mental health.

🔑 Why Peers? Shared language, shared experiences, less judgment. A peer educator in your class understands your reality better than any textbook.
🛠️ Skills of an Effective Peer Educator
SkillHow to Demonstrate It
Active ListeningEye contact, nodding, no phone, paraphrasing what you heard
Empathy"I can understand why you feel that way..."
KnowledgeKnow your topic well — be ready for tough questions
ConfidentialityNever share what someone tells you in a peer session
Non-judgmentNo one will share if they fear mockery or shame
ReferralKnow where to send someone for professional help
🇰🇪 Fatuma, Malindi: A Form 4 student trained by AMREF as a peer educator. When her classmate confided she was being harassed by a teacher, Fatuma listened without judgment, reassured her it wasn't her fault, and helped her report it to the school counsellor. "She trusted me because I didn't react with shock or gossip," Fatuma said.
⚠️ Limits of Peer Education

Peer educators are not counsellors or therapists. If someone reveals abuse, suicidal thoughts, pregnancy, or addiction — refer them to a qualified adult immediately. You are a bridge, not the destination.

⚠️ Mandatory Reporting: In Kenya, if a peer discloses child abuse or defilement, you are legally and morally obligated to report it to an authority — even if they ask you to keep it secret.
