🌱 Stages of Adolescent Development

Adolescence is the transition from childhood to adulthood, typically between ages 10–19. It is divided into three stages, each with distinct changes.

StageAgeKey Changes
Early Adolescence10–14Puberty begins, growth spurts, concrete thinking, peer influence grows
Middle Adolescence15–17Abstract thinking, identity exploration, romantic relationships, risk-taking
Late Adolescence18–19Full physical maturity, stable identity, future planning, greater independence
😊 Social & Emotional Changes

Adolescence is not just physical. The brain is undergoing massive rewiring — especially the prefrontal cortex (responsible for judgment) which isn't fully developed until age 25.

ChangeWhat it Looks Like
Mood swingsHappy one hour, crying the next — hormonal surges cause this
Identity formationExperimenting with style, values, beliefs — "Who am I?"
Peer importanceFriends matter more than family approval (temporarily)
Risk-takingTesting limits — can lead to positive (sport, art) or negative (drugs, reckless sex) experimentation
Romantic relationshipsFirst experiences of love, jealousy, heartbreak
Self-consciousnessIntense focus on body image and how others perceive you
🇰🇪 Ochieng, Kisumu: His mother complained he "changed overnight" at 15 — suddenly moody, obsessed with his appearance, and spending all his time with friends. His school counsellor explained: his brain is literally being rewired. These are normal developmental changes, not character flaws.
🧠 Semenarche & Wet Dreams

Semenarche is the first ejaculation in males, marking the beginning of reproductive capability. It typically occurs between ages 9–14 during puberty.

Wet dreams (nocturnal emissions) are involuntary ejaculations during sleep. They are completely normal, not a sign of illness or sin, and caused by hormonal changes during puberty. They occur in both males and females.

🔑 Normal & Healthy: Wet dreams are a normal part of puberty. They require no treatment and are not harmful. Many young people feel shame or confusion — this lesson aims to replace shame with accurate information.
