👩 Female Reproductive System
OrganFunction
OvariesProduce eggs (ova) and secrete oestrogen & progesterone
Fallopian TubesTransport egg from ovary to uterus; site of fertilisation
Uterus (Womb)Houses and nourishes the developing fetus
CervixLower part of uterus; dilates during childbirth
VaginaPassageway for menstrual flow, intercourse, and childbirth
VulvaExternal female genitalia
👨 Male Reproductive System
OrganFunction
TestesProduce sperm and testosterone
EpididymisStores and matures sperm; connects testes to vas deferens
Vas DeferensMuscular tube transporting sperm during ejaculation
Seminal VesiclesProduce fructose-rich fluid that energises sperm
Prostate GlandProduces fluid that nourishes and protects sperm in semen
UrethraCarries both urine AND semen out of the body (not simultaneously)
PenisDelivers sperm during intercourse; passes urine
🔑 Sperm Journey: Testes → Epididymis (matures, 64 days) → Vas Deferens → mixes with seminal fluid → Urethra → out during ejaculation
🔬 Fertilisation & Conception

Fertilisation occurs when one sperm penetrates and merges with an egg in the fallopian tube. The fertilised egg (zygote) travels to the uterus and implants in the lining — pregnancy begins. Sperm can survive inside the female body for up to 5 days.
