🤝 What Makes a Relationship Healthy?

Healthy relationships — with friends, family, or partners — are built on a foundation of respect, trust, and open communication. They help you grow; unhealthy relationships drain and harm you.

PillarWhat it Looks LikeWhat it Does NOT Look Like
RespectValuing each other's opinions and boundariesBelittling, mocking, controlling
CommunicationHonest, open conversations; listening activelyShouting, stonewalling, lying
TrustKeeping promises, being reliable, not checking their phoneJealousy, surveillance, suspicion
IndependenceEach person keeps their own friends and interestsIsolation from everyone else
EqualityBoth people's needs matter equallyOne person always decides everything
SafetyYou feel emotionally and physically safeFear, threats, violence
🇰🇪 Wanjiku & Kamau, Thika: Have been together since Form 3. They argue sometimes, but always talk it out calmly. Kamau still plays football with his friends; Wanjiku still goes to debate club. They encourage each other's dreams. Their friends describe them as a "healthy couple."
⚠️ Signs of an Unhealthy Relationship
⚠️ Warning signs you should never ignore:
  • Your partner checks your phone or controls who you talk to
  • You feel afraid to express your opinion
  • They put you down in front of others
  • They threaten you when you want to end the relationship
  • They isolate you from family and friends
  • Physical violence — even "just once"
🇰🇪 Akinyi, Kisumu: Her boyfriend Kevin demanded her phone password, deleted her male friends' contacts, and showed up at her school to "check on her." She mistook this for love. A school counsellor helped her understand these were signs of controlling, abusive behaviour — not love.
💬 Maintaining Healthy Peer Relationships
  • Active listening: Give full attention; don't plan your reply while they're still speaking
  • Empathy: Try to understand their feelings, not just their words
  • Conflict resolution: Address issues calmly and privately; avoid public confrontations
  • Boundaries: It is okay to say "I need some space" or "That makes me uncomfortable"
  • Positive influence: Build each other up; refuse to participate in gossip or bullying
