🧠 Understanding Stress & Mental Health in Kenya

According to the Kenya National Adolescent Mental Health Survey (K-NAMHS), 44.3% of Kenyan adolescents experienced a mental health problem in the past 12 months, and 12.2% met criteria for a mental disorder.

🔑 Stress: The body's natural response to any demand or challenge. It can be positive (eustress — e.g., preparing for a competition) or negative (distress — e.g., an abusive home situation).
Cause of StressHow it Affects Kenyan Teens

🇰🇪 Mercy, Kisii: A Form 3 student whose parents are going through a bitter divorce. She stopped eating properly, failed her mid-term exams, and started isolating herself. Her teacher referred her to the school counsellor who confirmed she was experiencing clinical depression from family stress.
⚡ Physical Signs of Stress

Stress triggers the body's "fight or flight" response — flooding the body with adrenaline and cortisol. Short-term this is helpful, but chronic stress causes physical damage.

  • Headaches and migraines
  • Stomach problems, nausea, ulcers
  • Chest tightness, rapid heartbeat
  • Insomnia or sleeping too much
  • Weakened immune system — getting sick more often
  • Skin problems (acne, rashes)
💪 Managing Stress — Healthy Coping Strategies
StrategyHow to Apply in Daily Life
Physical Exercise30 mins of football, running, or dancing releases stress hormones
Deep BreathingBreathe in 4 counts, hold 4, out 4 — resets your nervous system
Time ManagementBreak large tasks into small steps; use a timetable
Talk to SomeoneA trusted friend, parent, teacher, or counsellor
Limit Social MediaComparison on Instagram/TikTok intensifies stress
Sleep8–10 hours for teens — sleep deprivation multiplies stress
💡 When to Seek Help: If stress lasts more than 2 weeks, affects your eating/sleeping/schoolwork, or you feel hopeless — talk to a counsellor. Mental health is health.
