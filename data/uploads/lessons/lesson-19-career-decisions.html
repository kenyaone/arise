🎯 Making Career Decisions as a Teenager

Career decisions don't need to be final at 16 — but starting early gives you an advantage. Many Kenyans change careers multiple times. What matters is building a foundation of self-knowledge and skills.

🔑 Career Decision Framework:
1. Self-Assessment → 2. Explore Options → 3. Education/Training → 4. Decide & Plan → 5. Review & Adapt
🔎 Step 1: Know Yourself First
QuestionWhy it Matters
What activities do I enjoy most?Interest-aligned careers feel less like work
What am I naturally good at?Strengths predict success and satisfaction
What are my values?Career must align with what matters to you
Do I prefer working with people, data, or things?Determines right industry and role type
🇰🇪 Grace, Eldoret: Always loved biology but assumed she'd become a nurse because "that's what girls do." After a career assessment exercise at school, she discovered she had strong analytical skills and interest in research. She is now studying Biomedical Science at Moi University — a field she never knew existed at 14.

💡 TVET in Kenya: Technical and Vocational Education and Training centres like MTTI offer practical skills that lead directly to employment — often faster and at lower cost than university. Plumbers and electricians in Kenya earn more than many degree holders.
