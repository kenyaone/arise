🌿 What is Cannabis (Bhang)?

Cannabis sativa, commonly called bhang, ganja, or weed, is a plant whose active compounds — called cannabinoids — cause powerful effects on the brain and body. The two main compounds are THC and CBD.

🔑 THC (Tetrahydrocannabinol): The compound responsible for the "high" — altered thinking, hallucinations, and euphoria.
🔑 CBD (Cannabidiol): Has some medical uses (anti-inflammatory) but also contributes to addiction.
⚠️ Legal Status in Kenya: Cannabis is illegal in Kenya. Possession can lead to arrest, fines, and imprisonment under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act.
🧠 Effects on the Brain & Behaviour

🇰🇪 Baraka, Mombasa: A Form 4 student who started using bhang at 16 "to cope with exam pressure." By Form 4, he could barely sit through a lesson without his mind wandering. He failed his KCSE exams and says he cannot remember whole weeks of that year.
🫁 Physical Effects
  • Respiratory damage: Smoking bhang causes chronic bronchitis and increases lung cancer risk
  • Heart rate increase: THC temporarily raises heart rate — risky for those with heart conditions
  • Red eyes & dry mouth: Blood vessels in eyes dilate; saliva production decreases
  • Nausea reduction: CBD can reduce chemo-induced nausea (medical use only, under prescription)
⚠️ Long-Term & Special Risks

🇰🇪 Zawadi, Nairobi: A 20-year-old who started using bhang at 14. She now hears voices and was diagnosed with cannabis-induced psychosis. Her family spent their savings on psychiatric treatment. "We thought it was just a phase," her mother said.
