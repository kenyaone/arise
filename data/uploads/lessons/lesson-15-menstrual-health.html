🩸 The Menstrual Cycle Explained

Menstruation is a normal, healthy biological process. The average cycle is 28 days (range: 21–45 days in teens), regulated by four key hormones.

PhaseDaysWhat Happens
Menstrual Phase1–5Uterine lining sheds (period). Lasts 3–7 days.
Follicular Phase1–13FSH stimulates egg follicles; oestrogen rebuilds lining
Ovulation~14LH surge releases mature egg — MOST FERTILE point
Luteal Phase15–28Progesterone prepares lining. If no pregnancy: cycle resets
🔑 Key Hormones: FSH → grows follicles | Oestrogen → rebuilds lining | LH → triggers ovulation | Progesterone → prepares for pregnancy
🚿 Menstrual Hygiene — Why It Matters

Poor menstrual hygiene management causes real health problems — and also affects girls' education and dignity.

Risk of Poor HygieneHow it Happens
UTI (Urinary Tract Infection)Not changing pads regularly allows bacteria to grow
Bacterial VaginosisDisruption of vaginal flora from poor hygiene
Skin rashesProlonged contact with damp, soiled materials
PID (Pelvic Inflammatory Disease)Untreated infections spread to reproductive organs
School absenceLack of sanitary products or private toilets
🇰🇪 Zawadi, Turkana: Used to miss 3–4 school days every month because her school had no private toilets or water. She used old cloth that caused infections. After her school received a sanitary pad programme from the government, her attendance became consistent and her grades improved. Access to menstrual hygiene is a right.
