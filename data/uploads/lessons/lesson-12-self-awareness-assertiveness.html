🪞 Self-Awareness — Knowing Yourself

Self-awareness is the ability to honestly recognise your own emotions, thoughts, strengths, weaknesses, and the impact your behaviour has on others. It is the foundation of all personal growth.

🔑 Self-Awareness includes: Emotional awareness (recognising your feelings) | Accurate self-assessment (knowing your true strengths and weaknesses) | Self-confidence (trusting your own worth).
Low Self-AwarenessHigh Self-Awareness
Blames others for everythingTakes responsibility for own actions
Doesn't know their triggersKnows what makes them angry/anxious and why
Overestimates or underestimates abilitiesHas realistic view of strengths and weaknesses
Reacts impulsivelyPauses, reflects, then responds

How to build self-awareness: Keep a journal. Ask trusted people for honest feedback. Practise mindfulness — pause before reacting. Reflect on what went well and what didn't each day.

🇰🇪 Mwende, Machakos: Always got into arguments in class. Through journaling, she realised she got angry when she felt her ideas weren't respected. Once she understood her trigger, she started raising her hand calmly instead of snapping — and people actually started listening more.
🦁 Assertiveness — Speaking Up Respectfully

Assertiveness means expressing your needs, feelings, and boundaries directly, honestly, and respectfully — without aggression and without being a pushover.

StyleHow They ActResult
PassiveNever says what they need; agrees to everythingResentment, stress, others take advantage
AggressiveDemands, shouts, threatens to get their wayConflict, damaged relationships
Assertive ✓States needs calmly and clearly; listens to othersRespect, healthy relationships, reduced stress
🔑 Assertive Formula: "I feel [emotion] when [situation] because [reason]. I need [specific request]."
Example: "I feel uncomfortable when you touch my hair without asking. Please ask first."
🇰🇪 Baraka, Mombasa: His uncle kept asking him to skip school to help at the shop. He used to say yes even though he wanted to go to school. Learning assertiveness, he said: "Uncle, I value helping you but I need to attend school every day — my future depends on it. Can we arrange weekend help instead?" His uncle agreed and respected him more for it.
