🫁 Respiratory System

Every cigarette contains over 7,000 chemicals — 70 cause cancer. The lungs absorb these directly, making them the most damaged organ.

🔑 Carcinogen: A cancer-causing substance. Tobacco smoke contains tar, benzene and formaldehyde that damage lung cell DNA.

🇰🇪 Mwangi, Murang'a: Started smoking at Form 2 to fit in. At 40, he cannot walk to his shamba without stopping to catch his breath. His doctor diagnosed COPD — no cure exists. His children now do all the farm work.
❤️ Heart & Blood Vessels

Nicotine causes the heart to beat faster and raises blood pressure. Over time, blood vessel walls are poisoned, causing them to harden — a condition called atherosclerosis.

EffectRisk Compared to Non-Smokers

⚠️ Carbon monoxide in smoke replaces oxygen in blood. Your heart must work 3× harder after each cigarette.
🛡️ Immune System, Digestion & Skin

Smoking weakens immunity — smokers catch infections more easily and recover more slowly. TB spreads more readily in smokers. It also causes cancers of the mouth, throat, stomach and colon, and creates peptic ulcers that resist treatment.

🇰🇪 Akinyi, Kisumu: A 32-year-old teacher who looks 45. Her doctor links recurring stomach ulcers directly to her smoking habit. She has tried to quit three times.

📉 Life Expectancy & Cost

Smokers live an average of 10 years less than non-smokers. A pack-a-day habit costs up to KES 73,000 per year — enough for secondary school fees.

💡 Quitting reverses damage: 20 min → BP normalises | 48 hrs → Nerve endings regrow | 1 year → Heart disease risk −50% | 10 years → Lung cancer risk halved
