🤰 Causes of Early (Teen) Pregnancy in Kenya

Kenya has one of the highest rates of teenage pregnancy in East Africa. During the COVID-19 school closures alone, over 152,000 girls got pregnant. Understanding the causes is the first step to prevention.

CauseHow it Leads to Pregnancy
Lack of sex educationMyths about contraception; not knowing how pregnancy occurs
Peer pressurePressure to "prove maturity" through sex
PovertyGirls exchanging sex for money, food or school fees ("blessers")
Lack of parental supervisionNo one to guide or warn young people
Sexual abuse/coercionRape or deception by older men
Alcohol & drugsImpaired judgment leads to unprotected sex
🇰🇪 Zawadi, Kisumu: Dropped out of school in Form 2 at 15 after getting pregnant by a 32-year-old man who promised to pay her fees. "He said he loved me. I didn't understand that what he was doing was illegal. I didn't know what grooming was."
💔 Consequences of Teen Pregnancy
Area of ImpactSpecific Consequence
Health (Mother)Preeclampsia, anaemia, obstructed labour, higher maternal mortality
Health (Baby)Premature birth, low birth weight, developmental delays
EducationDropout rates — many girls never return to school
EconomicLifelong poverty cycle, dependence on family or social services
PsychologicalDepression, stigma, shame, isolation from peers
SocialStrained family relationships, loss of friendships
ChildHigher risk of poverty, lower academic achievement, repeating the cycle
⚠️ Kenya's Re-admission Policy: Since 2020, girls who get pregnant are legally allowed to return to school after giving birth. Refusing a girl re-entry is illegal. Report violations to the school board or Ministry of Education.
🛡️ Prevention — What You Can Do
  • Stay in school — education is the strongest protective factor
  • Know the age of consent (18) and report adults who pressure you
  • Talk to a trusted adult if you feel unsafe
  • Understand that "sugar daddies/mamas" are predators, not providers
  • Access youth-friendly health services at your nearest health centre
