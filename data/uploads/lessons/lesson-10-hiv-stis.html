🩺 HIV/AIDS in Kenya

Kenya has made significant progress against HIV, but it remains a serious public health challenge. 1.4 million Kenyans live with HIV, with a prevalence rate of 3.7%.

🔑 Key Statistic: 41% of all new adult HIV infections in Kenya occur in the 15–24 age group. Young people are most at risk — and most preventable.
How HIV SpreadsRisk Level
Unprotected sexual intercourseMost common route in Kenya
Shared needles (injection drug use)Very high risk — direct blood contact
Mother to child (pregnancy/breastfeeding)Preventable with ARVs
Blood transfusion (unscreened)Very rare in Kenya (blood is screened)
Needlestick injury (healthcare workers)Low with protective equipment
⚠️ HIV is NOT spread by: Hugging, handshakes, sharing food, mosquito bites, toilet seats, or casual contact. Stigma based on these myths destroys lives.
🇰🇪 Highest HIV Counties: Homa Bay (15.2%), Kisumu (14.5%), Siaya (13.2%), Migori (9.7%). These areas have targeted prevention programmes — but young people across ALL counties must protect themselves.

🇰🇪 Odhiambo, Kisumu: Had no symptoms but tested positive for chlamydia at a youth clinic. He completed antibiotic treatment and informed his partner. "If I hadn't tested, I would never have known — and my partner could have developed serious complications."
🤝 Prevention & Peer Support in HIV
  • Test regularly: Know your status — HIV testing is free at all government health centres
  • Use condoms correctly: Every time, with every partner
  • ARVs work: People on treatment can live full, healthy lives and cannot transmit HIV (Undetectable = Untransmittable)
  • PrEP: Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis — a pill that prevents HIV in high-risk individuals
  • Support groups: Peer supporters help people adhere to treatment and fight stigma
💡 U=U: Undetectable = Untransmittable. A person on HIV treatment whose viral load is undetectable CANNOT transmit HIV sexually. This has revolutionised HIV prevention.
