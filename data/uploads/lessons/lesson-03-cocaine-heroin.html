💊 Cocaine — The Stimulant Trap

Cocaine is a powerful stimulant derived from the coca plant of South America. It creates an intense but short-lived high — pushing users to take more and more to maintain the feeling.

🔑 Stimulant: A drug that speeds up the central nervous system — increasing heart rate, energy, alertness and blood pressure temporarily.
FormHow UsedHigh Duration
Powdered cocaineSnorted through nose / injected15–30 minutes
Crack cocaineSmoked5–10 minutes (very intense)

Short-term effects: Euphoria, high energy, raised heart rate and BP, dilated pupils, decreased appetite.

Long-term effects: Heart attacks, strokes, severe anxiety/paranoia, hallucinations, nasal tissue destruction (snorting), addiction.

⚠️ Overdose Risk: Cocaine overdose causes cardiac arrest, seizures, and death — even in first-time users. There is no antidote.
🇰🇪 Derrick, Nairobi Westlands: A 24-year-old event promoter introduced to cocaine at parties. Within 8 months he was spending KES 15,000/week on cocaine. He had a heart attack at 26 and survived — barely. "Every line felt like the last one I'd need. It never was."
💉 Heroin — The Most Addictive Drug

Heroin is processed from morphine, extracted from poppy plants. It creates an intense euphoric "rush" that lasts only minutes — but the craving for more lasts a lifetime.

🔑 Dependence vs Addiction: Physical dependence means the body NEEDS the drug to function. Addiction means compulsive drug-seeking despite knowing the harm.
Health EffectDescription
Collapsed VeinsFrom repeated injection — veins are destroyed
Heart Valve Infection (Endocarditis)Bacteria from dirty needles infect the heart
HIV/Hepatitis B&CShared needles transmit deadly viruses
Liver & Kidney DiseaseOrgans fail under chronic heroin load
Overdose (Respiratory Depression)Breathing slows to a stop — fatal without naloxone
🇰🇪 Kamau, Mathare: Started on cheap heroin ("brown sugar") at 18, introduced by a neighbour. By 22, he had HIV from shared needles and endocarditis. He received treatment through a rehabilitation programme and is now a peer counsellor — warning others.
🩺 Treatment for Cocaine & Heroin Addiction
  • Behavioural therapy (CBT): Identifies triggers and builds coping strategies
  • Medication (heroin): Methadone and buprenorphine reduce cravings safely
  • Rehabilitation centres: Mathare Hospital, NACADA-approved centres across Kenya
  • Support groups: Narcotics Anonymous (NA) — free, meets in most Kenyan towns
💡 NACADA Kenya: The National Authority for the Campaign Against Alcohol and Drug Abuse. Free helpline: 1192
