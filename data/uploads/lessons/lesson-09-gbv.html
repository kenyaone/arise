⚖️ What is Gender-Based Violence (GBV)?

GBV refers to harmful acts directed at a person because of their gender. It is rooted in inequality, abuse of power, and harmful cultural norms. It affects anyone, but disproportionately affects women and girls.

🇰🇪 Naipanoi, Kajiado: Was to undergo FGM at 13 as part of Maasai cultural transition. Her teacher and the Girls Not Brides organisation helped her understand her rights. She reported the plan to authorities and was protected. She is now in Form 3 and a community advocate.
🔍 Causes of GBV
  • Gender inequality: Societies that treat women as inferior create environments where GBV is normalised
  • Cultural norms: Practices that enforce male dominance (FGM, child marriage, bride price)
  • Economic inequality: Women dependent on men cannot easily leave abusive situations
  • Weak justice systems: Perpetrators face no consequences, encouraging repeat abuse
  • Substance abuse: Alcohol and drugs reduce impulse control and increase aggression
  • Media: Content that portrays violence against women as normal or entertaining
