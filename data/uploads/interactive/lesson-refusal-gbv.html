🚫 What Is GBV and Why Refusal Skills MatterGBV ni Nini na Kwa Nini Ujuzi wa Kukataa UnamaanishaGBV ni Nini na Kwa Nini Refusal Skills Zinamaanisha
🔑 Definition: Gender-Based Violence (GBV)🔑 Maana: Ukatili wa Kijinsia (GBV)🔑 Definition: Gender-Based Violence (GBV) GBV is any harmful act directed at a person because of their gender. In Kenyan adolescent contexts this includes: physical violence by partners, sexual coercion, emotional/psychological abuse, FGM, forced marriage, and online harassment. GBV ni tendo lolote la madhara linaloelekezwa kwa mtu kwa sababu ya jinsia yake. Katika mazingira ya vijana Kenya, hii ni pamoja na: jeuri ya kimwili na mwenzi, kulazimishwa kingono, unyanyasaji wa kihisia/kisaikolojia, ukeketaji, ndoa ya kulazimishwa, na unyanyasaji mtandaoni. GBV ni any harmful act inayoelekezwa kwa mtu kwa sababu ya gender yao. Kwa Kenyan adolescent contexts hii ni pamoja na: physical violence na partners, sexual coercion, emotional/psychological abuse, FGM, forced marriage, na online harassment.
🔑 Key Term: Coercive Control🔑 Neno Muhimu: Udhibiti wa Kulazimisha🔑 Key Term: Coercive Control A pattern of behaviour used to dominate, isolate, and control a partner. It is often invisible at first but escalates over time. Mfumo wa tabia unaotumiwa kutawala, kutengwa, na kudhibiti mwenzi. Mara nyingi hauonekani mwanzoni lakini unakuwa mbaya zaidi kadri muda unavyopita. Pattern ya behaviour inayotumiwa ku-dominate, ku-isolate, na ku-control partner. Mara nyingi haionekani mwanzoni lakini inaescalate over time.

KDHS 2022: 34% ya wanawake Kenya wenye umri wa miaka 15–49 wamepata jeuri ya kimwili. GBV HAIZUII wanawake peke yao — wavulana na wanaume pia wanaweza kuwa waathirika. KDHS 2022: 34% ya wanawake Kenya wenye umri wa miaka 15–49 wamepitia physical violence. GBV HAIFANYIKI kwa wanawake tu — wavulana na wanaume pia wanaweza kuwa victims.

Scenario: Fatuma, 15, lives with her uncle after her parents died. He has started coming into her room at night and touching her inappropriately. He tells her: "If you tell anyone, no one will believe you and you'll be sent to an orphanage."
What should Fatuma do?
✅
Correct — Tell a Trusted Adult

Fatuma is being abused and threatened into silence — this is a classic pattern of child sexual abuse. Teachers and school counsellors are mandatory reporters in Kenya — they are required to act. What her uncle said is a lie: authorities will believe Fatuma, and she will not be punished. Telling is the only way to stop the abuse.

❌
Silence Enables the Abuse to Continue

Abuse rarely stops on its own — it usually escalates. Every day of silence is a day the abuser remains safe and Fatuma remains at risk. The threat ("no one will believe you") is a control tactic designed to prevent exactly what should happen: telling. Staying silent protects the abuser, not Fatuma.

✅
Excellent — Childline Is There for This

Childline 116 is free, confidential, and available 24 hours. Counsellors are trained to handle exactly this situation. Fatuma can call from any phone, including her uncle's, without him knowing why she called. She will be believed, supported, and protected — not punished.

⚠️
This Could Be Dangerous

Confronting an abuser directly — especially one with power over you — can trigger more severe abuse or threats. Fatuma is a minor in a vulnerable position. The safer path is always to tell someone outside the household who has authority to act: a teacher, counsellor, or Childline 116. Do not confront the abuser alone.
