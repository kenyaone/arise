Scenario: Brian, 17, has lost KES 1,200 betting on SportPesa this week — money he saved from doing chores. His friend says: "Don't stop now, you're so close to winning it back. Put in 500 more and your luck will turn."
What should Brian do?
✅
Recognises the Trap — Best Decision

Brian correctly identifies "chasing losses" — the most dangerous gambling behaviour. The feeling that you're "almost winning" is manufactured by betting algorithms. In reality, each bet is independent — past losses don't make future wins more likely. The house always wins long-term. Brian walked away with KES 1,200 gone, but stopped the bleeding before it got worse.

Brian loses again — now KES 1,700 down. His friend says "Double up — it has to come in now." What does Brian do?
⚠️
Stopped — But Already Deep in Losses

Brian stopped, which is good. But he now owes KES 1,700 and learned an expensive lesson about the chasing-losses trap. Key lesson: The right time to stop is always at the first loss. Every additional bet in a losing streak is driven by emotion, not logic. The odds never improve because you've already lost.

❌
Debt Trap — Addiction Pattern

Brian is now borrowing money to gamble — a clear sign of gambling addiction. This pattern escalates: stolen money, broken friendships, school dropout. NACADA reports gambling as the fastest-growing addiction among Kenyan youth aged 15–24. Call NACADA free helpline: 1192. It is confidential.

✅
Self-Awareness — Seeking Help Early

Brian recognises that the urge to keep chasing losses is a sign of a gambling problem, not a lucky streak. Talking to someone — a trusted adult, counsellor, or the NACADA helpline (1192) — early prevents the problem from spiralling. Early intervention has an 80%+ success rate. This took real courage and self-awareness.

✅
Strong Practical Step

Removing access is one of the most effective strategies for breaking a gambling habit. Urges are strongest in the first 72 hours — if the app is gone, the impulse cannot be acted on. Brian should also tell a trusted adult what happened, so he has accountability and support going forward.
