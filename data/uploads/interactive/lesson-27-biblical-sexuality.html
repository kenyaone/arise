💕 Sexuality as God's Design
🔑 Human Sexuality The physical, emotional, relational, and spiritual dimensions of being male or female.

Genesis 1:27 — "God created mankind in his own image... male and female he created them."

Genesis 2:24 — "A man leaves his father and mother and is united to his wife, and they become one flesh."

The Bible presents sexuality as good and created by God — but for a specific purpose: intimate union within marriage.

🇰🇪 Ukweli wa Imani
Mungu aliumba 'intimacy' — si kitu chafu. Ni gift, lakini ina boundaries.
💡 Tip Sexuality is not just about sex — it includes how we relate to others, our emotions, and our sense of identity.
💒 God's Design for Sex: Within Marriage

The Bible consistently places sexual intimacy within the covenant of marriage — one man, one woman, lifelong commitment.

1 Corinthians 7:2 — "Each man should have sexual relations with his own wife, and each woman with her own husband."

Why marriage? It provides security, commitment, and the best environment for children.

📖 Joseph's Integrity (Genesis 39) Joseph refused the advances of Potiphar's wife — choosing integrity over temptation at personal cost. He was imprisoned for doing the right thing, yet God honoured his faithfulness.
⚠️ Real Consequences Sex outside marriage exposes you to STIs, unplanned pregnancy, emotional hurt, and spiritual harm — consequences God's design protects you from.
✨ Sexual Purity and Abstinence

Abstinence means choosing not to engage in sexual activity until marriage.

1 Thessalonians 4:3 — "It is God's will that you should be sanctified: that you should avoid sexual immorality."

Purity is not just physical — it includes thoughts, media, and relationships.

Practical tips for staying pure:

  • Set clear relationship boundaries with your partner early
  • Avoid situations that tempt you (being alone late at night)
  • Fill your mind with positive content
  • Have accountability — a trusted friend or mentor
  • Pray for strength and discipline

💌 Healthy God-Honoring Relationships

A God-honoring relationship respects boundaries, builds character, and prepares for marriage.

🔑 Covenant A deep, binding commitment made before God — the foundation of marriage.

Signs of a healthy, values-based relationship:

  • Both people respect each other's boundaries without pressure
  • The relationship draws both people closer to God and their goals
  • There is honesty, kindness, and mutual encouragement
  • No one uses the other person for physical satisfaction only
📖 Ruth and Boaz (Book of Ruth) A story of mutual respect, integrity, and God's blessing on a righteous relationship. Boaz honoured Ruth publicly and acted with integrity at every step.
💡 Tip If a relationship consistently pulls you away from your faith, family, or goals — it is not God's best for you.
📵 Pornography and Lust: A Modern Danger

Matthew 5:28 — "Anyone who looks at a woman lustfully has already committed adultery with her in his heart."

Pornography is harmful because it:

  • Distorts healthy views of sex and relationships
  • Creates addiction affecting the brain like drugs
  • Objectifies women and men
  • Creates unrealistic expectations for marriage

Online pornography is increasingly available to Kenyan youth via smartphones.

⚠️ You Are Not Alone If you struggle with pornography or lustful thoughts, you are not alone — and you can get help. Talk to a trusted pastor, counsellor, or mentor.
🇰🇪 Ukweli wa Mitaani
Kama unajaribu kutoka kwa tabia mbaya ya mtandaoni, usijaribu peke yako — tafuta msaada.
💕 Forgiveness and Restoration

The Bible is clear that no sexual sin is beyond God's forgiveness.

1 John 1:9 — "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness."

Secondary virginity — making a fresh commitment to sexual purity after past mistakes.

Steps to restoration:

  1. Acknowledge what happened — be honest with yourself and God
  2. Confess and ask for forgiveness (from God and others where appropriate)
  3. Make a new commitment to purity going forward
  4. Seek support from a trusted counsellor, pastor, or mentor
📖 David — Psalm 51 Despite serious moral failure, David confessed honestly before God, was forgiven, and was restored to relationship with God. His psalm of repentance is one of the most powerful in Scripture.
💡 Tip You are not defined by your past. God's grace is greater than your mistakes.

Scenario: Samuel, 17, is challenged by his friends: "Your religion says wait — but this is Kenya, everyone is doing it. You're hiding behind the Bible. Are you scared? Prove you're a man."
What should Samuel do?
A friend says "Your church friends aren't here — stop pretending." How does Samuel respond?
✅
Integrity — Best Outcome

Samuel's response defines character: living by values consistently, not performing them for an audience. This response also deflates the social pressure by removing the audience's power over him. Research consistently shows that adolescents with a clear, internalised reason for abstinence — whether faith-based, personal, or practical — have better health outcomes than those relying on willpower alone.

⚠️
Removed Himself — Safe Choice

Leaving without arguing was wise. Samuel didn't need to win the debate — he just needed to not lose the decision. Protecting your values does not require the other person's agreement.

The argument goes in circles. A girl joins and says "I think guys who wait are actually more attractive." One of Samuel's friends pressures him: "Don't let her change you." What now?
✅
Stood Firm and Ended It

Samuel closed the loop: he named his consistency, ended the argument, and moved on without making a scene. This is mature conflict resolution. The social pressure lost because it found nothing to push against.

⚠️
Separated Himself from the Group Dynamic

Talking privately, away from the group performance pressure, is a legitimate choice. The pressure to "prove manhood" disappears when the audience is gone. Samuel remains responsible for his own values in that private conversation too.

❌
A Statistical Lie and a Social Trap

"Everyone is doing it" is factually wrong. Studies of Kenyan adolescents consistently show that between 40-60% of secondary school students report being sexually inactive — but most believe they are in the minority because those who abstain don't talk about it. Samuel was being asked to make a permanent, high-stakes decision (risk of pregnancy, STIs, his own values) based on a false premise. His values are not a weakness. They are a protection.
