Scenario: Diana, 16, is approached by a wealthy man in his 40s who offers to pay her school fees: "You're intelligent and beautiful. I'll take care of your fees, your phone, everything — just be my 'special friend.' No one has to know."
What should Diana do?
Diana's friend says "Don't be stupid — he's rich and he's offering. Everyone does it." What does Diana say?
✅
Chose Her Future — Best Outcome

Diana identified this correctly: transactional sex relationships rarely stay "just financial." They escalate to sexual pressure and coercion, with pregnancy and STI risk following. The Constituency Bursary Fund, school hardship funds, and NGO scholarships are real alternatives that don't trade her body for an education.

⚠️
Held Her Ground Quietly

Avoiding was right. Next step: ask a teacher or school counsellor about legitimate fee support options. The problem (fees) is real and deserves a real solution.

The man makes clear what "special friend" means and offers twice as much money. What now?
✅
Got Out and Sought Help

Diana realised "hear more" became "accept or refuse." She chose to leave and report. This is the right sequence: protect yourself, then tell someone who can help and potentially stop this man from targeting other girls.

❌
Fees Paid, Future Risked

Transactional sex carries pregnancy and STI risk with every encounter. The power imbalance means Diana cannot say no once she has accepted money. In Kenya, girls who leave school due to pregnancy lose an average of 9 years of income. The money offered never compensates for that.

❌
A Short-Term Solution With Long-Term Consequences

Transactional relationships with older men are a leading driver of teenage pregnancy in Kenya. The power imbalance means Diana cannot negotiate safe sex or walk away easily once she has accepted money. School fees can be replaced. Her health, education, and future cannot. Options exist: bursary funds, Equity Bank Wings to Fly, Uwezo Fund.
