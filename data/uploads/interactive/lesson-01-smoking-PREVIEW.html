🔍 Key Inquiry Question: Why do people continue smoking even when they know it kills — and what exactly does it do inside the body?

🫁 The Respiratory System — Worst Hit

Every cigarette contains over 7,000 chemicals — 70 of which are confirmed carcinogens. The lungs absorb these directly with every breath of smoke, making them the most damaged organ.

🔑 Carcinogen: A cancer-causing chemical. Tobacco smoke contains tar, benzene, and formaldehyde that directly damage lung cell DNA, triggering uncontrolled cell growth.
ConditionWhat HappensMain Symptoms
Lung CancerCarcinogens damage DNA; cells grow uncontrollablyChronic cough, blood in sputum, chest pain
Chronic BronchitisAirways permanently inflamed and clogged with mucusPersistent cough, wheezing, breathlessness
EmphysemaAir sacs (alveoli) permanently destroyed — no gas exchangeSevere breathlessness, cannot walk or exercise
🇰🇪 Mwangi, Murang'a: Started smoking at Form 2 "to fit in." At 40, he cannot walk to his shamba without stopping to catch his breath. His doctor diagnosed COPD — there is no cure. His children now do all the farm work while he watches from a chair by the door.

❤️ Heart & Blood Vessels

Nicotine causes the heart to beat faster and raises blood pressure with every single cigarette. Over time, blood vessel walls are damaged and hardened — a condition called atherosclerosis.

🔑 Atherosclerosis: Build-up of fatty plaques that narrow and harden arteries. Smoking is a major trigger — the process begins in teenagers who smoke regularly.
Cardiovascular RiskCompared to Non-SmokersHow It Happens
Heart Attack2–4× higherBlocked coronary arteries from plaque
Stroke2–3× higherBlood clots travel to brain arteries
High Blood PressureRaised every cigaretteNicotine constricts blood vessels
Limb Amputation (PAD)6× higherSevere blood flow reduction to legs
⚠️ Carbon monoxide in smoke replaces oxygen in red blood cells. Your heart must work 3× harder after each cigarette to supply adequate oxygen to the body.

🛡️ Immune System, Digestion & Skin

Smoking is not just a lung disease — it attacks every body system simultaneously.

Body SystemEffect of Smoking
Immune SystemWeakened — more infections, slower recovery, 3× higher TB risk
DigestiveCancer of mouth, throat, oesophagus, stomach, colon; peptic ulcers
SkinPremature wrinkles, dull complexion, slow wound healing
Oral HealthSevere gum disease, tooth loss, mouth cancer, persistent bad breath
BonesOsteoporosis — reduced bone density, increased fracture risk
🇰🇪 Akinyi, Kisumu: A 32-year-old teacher constantly mistaken for 45. Her doctor links recurring stomach ulcers directly to her 14-year smoking habit. "My students once asked if I was the headteacher's mother." She has tried to quit three times.
💡 Did You Know? Smokers are 3× more likely to develop tuberculosis (TB). Smoking destroys the tiny cilia (hair-like structures) that protect airways from bacteria — leaving lungs wide open to infection.

🇰🇪 Nasieku, Kajiado: Both she and her husband smoked during pregnancy. Their son was born 7 weeks premature and spent 3 weeks in NICU. She quit immediately after. "Every scan, the doctor showed me how my baby was getting less oxygen. I wish someone had told me this the day I lit my first cigarette."
⚠️ Smoking during pregnancy is the single most preventable cause of premature birth and infant death in Kenya. There is no "safe" level of smoking during pregnancy.

📉 Life Expectancy, Cost & Quitting

Smokers live on average 10 years less than non-smokers. The earlier you start, the more life-years you lose. But quitting at ANY age improves health immediately.

Time After QuittingWhat Recovers
20 minutesBlood pressure and heart rate normalise
48 hoursCarbon monoxide leaves blood; nerve endings begin regrowing
2 weeksCirculation improves; walking becomes easier
1 yearHeart disease risk drops by 50%
10 yearsLung cancer risk halved; stroke risk equals a non-smoker
💰 Financial Cost in Kenya: A pack costs KES 50–200. A pack-a-day habit costs up to KES 73,000/year — enough to pay secondary school fees, buy a dairy cow, or save for land.

Resources for quitting: NACADA helpline 1192 (free), your doctor, or a pharmacist for nicotine replacement therapy (NRT).

🧠 Nicotine Addiction — Why Quitting Is Hard

Understanding why people start — and why they struggle to stop — is key to genuine prevention.

🔑 Nicotine Addiction: Nicotine floods the brain with dopamine — the "reward" chemical. Within weeks of regular smoking, the brain requires nicotine just to feel normal. Stopping causes withdrawal: irritability, anxiety, difficulty concentrating, insomnia.
Reason for StartingThe Reality
Peer pressure / "fitting in"Friends who truly care do not pressure you to harm yourself
Stress reliefNicotine temporarily masks stress — worsens anxiety long-term
"Looks cool / adult"Tobacco companies deliberately target teenagers with this image
CuriosityOne cigarette creates measurable brain changes in dopamine craving
⚠️ Tobacco Marketing in Kenya: Tobacco companies specifically target young Kenyans with affordable single cigarettes and flavoured products. They know that 90% of adult smokers started before age 18.

🏛️ Second-Hand Smoke & Kenya's Tobacco Laws

Second-hand smoke is nearly as dangerous as smoking directly. Non-smokers who regularly inhale someone else's smoke face 80–90% of the same cancer and cardiovascular risks.

  • Children with smoking parents have 40% higher asthma risk
  • Partners of smokers have 30% higher heart disease risk
  • Second-hand smoke contains the same 70 carcinogenic chemicals
  • There is no safe level of exposure to tobacco smoke
🇰🇪 Tobacco Control Act 2007:
  • Smoking banned in all public places, workplaces, and public transport
  • Selling cigarettes to anyone under 18 is a criminal offence
  • Graphic health warnings mandatory on all tobacco packaging
  • Penalties: fines up to KES 500,000 or 2 years imprisonment

📝 Practice Problems

Question 1: Musa smokes 10 cigarettes a day at KES 15 each. Calculate: (a) weekly cost, (b) monthly cost, (c) annual cost. What could be bought with the annual amount?

Solution:
(a) Daily: 10 × 15 = KES 150 | Weekly: 150 × 7 = KES 1,050
(b) Monthly: 150 × 30 = KES 4,500
(c) Annual: 150 × 365 = KES 54,750 — enough for Form 1–2 school fees at most county schools, or a quality goat every 2 weeks.
Question 2: Name THREE body systems damaged by smoking (apart from the lungs) and give one specific disease or condition per system.

Model Answer:
1. Cardiovascular → Heart attack (2–4× higher risk from atherosclerosis)
2. Digestive → Stomach cancer and peptic ulcers (from carcinogen damage to mucosa)
3. Immune → Tuberculosis — 3× higher risk because cilia are destroyed, allowing bacteria into airways
Question 3: Your younger brother says "I only smoke 2 on weekends — that can't hurt." Using evidence, how would you respond?

Model Answer: Even 2 cigarettes causes measurable arterial damage and nicotine-induced brain changes. Addiction typically begins with "weekend-only" use. One cigarette can trigger cravings in genetically susceptible individuals. Lung damage is cumulative — there is no safe threshold.
