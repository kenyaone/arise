🚫 Understanding FGM and Why It Must Be RefusedKuelewa FGM na Kwa Nini Lazima IkataliweKuelewa FGM na Kwa Nini Ni Lazima Ikataliwe
What is FGM?FGM ni nini?FGM ni nini? Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) is the partial or total removal of the female external genitalia for non-medical reasons. It is practiced in some communities as a rite of passage but is recognised globally as a violation of human rights. Ukeketaji wa Kike (FGM) ni kuondolewa kwa sehemu au yote ya sehemu za nje za uzazi wa mwanamke kwa sababu zisizo za matibabu. Inafanywa katika baadhi ya jamii kama sherehe ya mpito lakini inatambuliwa kimataifa kama ukiukaji wa haki za binadamu. FGM ni kukata sehemu au yote ya sehemu za nje za mwanamke kwa reasons ambazo si za matibabu. Inafanywa kwa baadhi ya communities kama rite of passage lakini globally inatambuliwa kama ukiukaji wa human rights.

Immediate and long-term harms include:Madhara ya haraka na ya muda mrefu ni pamoja na:Madhara ya haraka na ya muda mrefu ni pamoja na:

  • Severe pain, bleeding, and infectionMaumivu makali, kutoka damu, na maambukiziMaumivu makali, kutoka damu, na infection
  • Risk of HIV transmission from shared instrumentsHatari ya maambukizi ya VVU kutoka kwa vyombo vilivyoshirikiwaRisk ya HIV kutoka kwa vyombo vilivyoshirikiwa
  • Complications in childbirth and lifelong painMatatizo wakati wa kujifungua na maumivu ya maisha yoteComplications wakati wa kujifungua na maumivu ya maisha yote
  • Psychological trauma — anxiety, PTSD, depressionMsongo wa mawazo — wasiwasi, PTSD, unyogovuPsychological trauma — anxiety, PTSD, depression
⚠ No cultural tradition can justify permanent harm to a child's body.⚠ Hakuna mila ya kitamaduni inayoweza kuhalalisha kudhuru mwili wa mtoto kwa kudumu.⚠ Hakuna cultural tradition inayoweza ku-justify kudumu kwa damage kwa mwili wa mtoto.

Tradition / Culture Pressure "It's our culture — a girl who refuses is not a real woman." This uses shame and identity to override your rights. Inatumia shame na identity ku-override haki zako.
Marriage Threat "No man will marry you if you are not cut." This uses fear of social exclusion to force compliance. "Hakuna mwanaume atakayekuoa kama hujakeketwa." Hii inatumia hofu ya kutengwa na jamii kulazimisha utiifu. "Hakuna mwanaume atakaokuoa kama hujakeketwa." Inatumia fear ya social exclusion ku-force compliance.
Family Pressure "Your grandmother and mother went through it — you will too." Generational pressure normalises harm. "Bibi yako na mama yako walipitia — wewe pia utapita." Shinikizo la vizazi vinafanya madhara ionekane ya kawaida. "Bibi yako na mama yako walipitia — wewe pia utapita." Generational pressure inafanya harm ionekane normal.
Secrecy and Surprise Some girls are taken without being told. Understanding this tactic means asking trusted adults about any sudden travel plans. Wasichana wengine huchukuliwa bila kujulishwa. Kuelewa mbinu hii kunamaanisha kuuliza watu wazima wanaokuamini kuhusu mipango yoyote ya ghafla ya safari. Wasichana wengine hupelekwa bila kujulishwa. Scenario: Naomi, 14, has been told by her grandmother that during the school holiday she will undergo a "special ceremony." Her older sister whispers that it involves cutting. Naomi is afraid but her mother says "It is our tradition — every girl in this family has done it."
What should Naomi do?
✅
Correct — This Is a Medical Emergency

FGM is illegal in Kenya (Prohibition of FGM Act 2011) and a form of child abuse. Teachers are mandatory reporters — they must act. Naomi telling her teacher can stop the procedure before it happens. There is protection available: safe houses, legal intervention, and counselling for the family. Naomi's body is her own. No tradition overrides the law.

✅
Excellent — Childline Will Act Immediately

Childline 116 is available 24 hours, free, and confidential. They handle FGM risk cases and can arrange immediate intervention and safe placement if needed. Naomi should call as soon as possible — before the holiday, not after. She can call even from her family's phone. She does not have to give her full name immediately.

❌
Permanent Harm — Tradition Does Not Make It Safe

FGM causes permanent physical harm: severe pain, bleeding, infection, complications in childbirth, and lifelong sexual and psychological consequences. It is illegal in Kenya regardless of tradition, religion, or who performs it. A practice being traditional does not make it right or safe. Naomi has the legal right to refuse — and to be protected.

⚠️
Dangerous — Get Official Help Instead

Running away leaves Naomi without protection and potentially in greater danger. The safer path is to get official help before the holiday: tell a teacher, call Childline 116, or contact the nearest police station. These authorities can intervene legally without Naomi having to flee alone. She deserves protection — not isolation.
