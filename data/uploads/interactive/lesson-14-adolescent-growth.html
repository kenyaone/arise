🧠 🧠 Brain Development
The brain undergoes massive rewiring during adolescence. The prefrontal cortex (decision-making, impulse control) is the last part to fully develop — not complete until age 25. This explains why teenagers sometimes take risks.
📈 📈 Height & Bone Growth
Growth hormone surges trigger a growth spurt — girls typically experience it at 10–14, boys at 12–16. Long bones lengthen rapidly. Calcium intake during this period is critical for strong bones throughout life.
💛 💛 Chest & Body Shape
Girls: breast development (thelarche) — often the first visible sign of puberty. Boys: shoulders broaden, chest widens, muscle mass increases due to testosterone. Both sexes develop pubic and underarm hair.
🔴 🔴 Voice & Hormones
Boys: the larynx grows rapidly causing voice breaking. Both sexes: hormones (oestrogen and testosterone) rise sharply, driving all physical changes. Mood swings are partly caused by hormone fluctuations.
🟣 🟣 Reproductive Development
Girls: menarche (first period), usually 2–3 years after breast development. Boys: testicular growth, sperm production begins, spontaneous erections. Both become biologically capable of reproduction.
🔵 🔵 Skin Changes
Sebaceous glands become more active (testosterone-driven), producing more oil. This causes acne — very common in both boys and girls. Sweat glands also increase activity, requiring daily hygiene.

Scenario: Mercy, 14, is approached by Mr. Kamau, a 34-year-old man, on her way home from school: "You've grown up so beautifully. Let me take you for lunch — I'll buy you new clothes and a phone. Don't tell your parents, they won't understand our friendship."
What should Mercy do?
Mercy's mother says "He could just be a kind adult." Mercy shows her the phone number he gave. What should they do?
✅
Reported — Best Possible Response

Mercy and her mother did exactly right. This man's approach — gifts, secrecy from parents, complimenting a child's physical development — is textbook grooming. Providing the number to police creates a record that may protect other girls. Sexual contact with anyone under 18 is statutory defilement in Kenya — regardless of gifts or "friendship." Well done, Mercy.

⚠️
Safe For Now — But Incomplete

Blocking was the right first step. However, without a report, this man remains free to approach other girls in the area. If Mercy feels safe enough, a report to the Children's Officer or school safeguarding lead is the next right step.

He finds Mercy again three days later and says "I've been thinking about you. Let me just buy you lunch — nothing more." What now?
✅
Recognised the Pattern and Got Help

A second approach after avoidance is a warning sign, not a sign of harmlessness. Mercy's instinct to get help was correct. This escalation pattern — repeated, persistent contact after a child has shown discomfort — is how grooming operates before abuse.

❌
Grooming Completed Its First Stage

The "just lunch" framing is stage one of grooming: establish comfort, create obligation, escalate. Girls aged 14-17 are the most targeted group for this form of exploitation in Kenya. The pregnancy and STI risk from these relationships is very high because the power imbalance means a girl cannot refuse or leave safely. Report: 1195 or Childline: 116.

❌
The Request for Secrecy Was the Warning

An adult who asks a child to hide a "friendship" from her parents is not a friend — he is a predator. The gifts are grooming tools, not generosity. Men who target young girls this way are the single largest external driver of teenage pregnancy in Kenya. Mercy's physical development is not an invitation. Report: 1195 (free, 24 hours).
