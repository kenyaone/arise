🔬 🔬 CD4 T-Cells (Immune System)
HIV targets and destroys CD4 T-cells — the immune system's commanders. A healthy person has 500–1500 CD4 cells/mm³. Below 200 = AIDS diagnosis. Without ARVs the immune system is progressively dismantled.
🧠 🧠 Brain
HIV crosses the blood-brain barrier causing HIV-associated neurocognitive disorder (HAND) — memory problems, confusion, mood changes. In late AIDS, opportunistic infections like cryptococcal meningitis attack the brain.
🫁 🫁 Lungs
PCP pneumonia (Pneumocystis jirovecii) is the most common AIDS-defining illness. Also at risk: tuberculosis (TB), which kills more HIV-positive people globally than any other infection.
🟤 🟤 Digestive System
Opportunistic infections cause chronic diarrhoea, weight loss, and malabsorption. Candida (thrush) attacks the mouth and oesophagus. Cryptosporidium causes severe persistent diarrhoea in AIDS patients.
🟣 🟣 Sexual Transmission
HIV passes through vaginal fluids, semen, blood and breast milk. Undetectable = Untransmittable (U=U): a person on effective ARVs with undetectable viral load cannot transmit HIV sexually.
💉 💉 Blood & Lymph System
HIV travels in the blood and lymphatic system. Lymph nodes swell as the immune system fights the virus. Blood transfusions (before 1986 screening) and shared needles are high-risk transmission routes.

✅
Boundary Held — Best Outcome

Esther named the manipulation directly. A partner who compares you unfavourably to "other girls" to get unprotected sex is demonstrating that this relationship prioritises his comfort over her health. HIV window periods mean a "clean" test from last year means nothing about today's status. Esther's rule is medically correct.

⚠️
Boundary Held — But Silently

Staying firm was right. Naming the manipulation out loud — "this is pressure, not love" — builds Esther's own confidence and makes future boundaries easier to hold.

He can't produce any results. He says "I told you I'm clean — isn't my word enough?" What now?
✅
Used Knowledge as Protection

Esther held firm on abstinence — the only 100% reliable protection. HIV window periods mean that a "negative" test from last year is not a guarantee today. Real partners respect each other's boundaries without pressure or ultimatums.

❌
Trust Became a Health Risk

Esther's guilt about "doubting" him was used against her. HIV can be transmitted by partners who genuinely believe they are negative. Unprotected sex also carries direct pregnancy risk. In Kenya, HIV rates among adolescent girls are 4× higher than boys. Choosing abstinence eliminates this risk entirely.

❌
Trust Without Protection Is a Health Risk

HIV testing has a window period — a person can test negative but still be infectious for weeks after exposure. Beyond HIV, unprotected sex carries direct pregnancy risk. Over 380,000 teenagers become pregnant in Kenya each year — "I trust him" is among the most common phrases in their stories. Trust your partner AND protect your health.
