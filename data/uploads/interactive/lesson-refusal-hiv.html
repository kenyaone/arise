🔄 Pressure Tactics That Increase STI RiskMbinu za Shinikizo Zinazoongeza Hatari ya Magonjwa ya ZinaaPressure Tactics Zinazoongeza STI Risk
1. “Abstinence is old-fashioned — no one waits any more”“Kujizuia ni cha kizamani — hakuna anayesubiri tena”“Abstinence ni ya kizamani — hakuna anaewait tena” Peer-normalising. What others do does not protect your health — your values do. Kufanya mawazo ya wenzako kuwa ya kawaida. Wenzako wanachofanya hakukulindi — maadili yako yanakuwalinda. Ku-normalise peer behaviour. Wenzako wanachofanya haiku-protect — maadili yako yanaku-protect.
2. “I was tested last year — I’m clean”“Nilifanyiwa kipimo mwaka jana — mimi ni safi”“Nilitest mwaka jana — niko clean” False assurance. HIV status changes with new exposures — a year-old test means nothing today. Uhakika wa uongo. Hali ya VVU hubadilika na mfiduo mpya — kipimo cha mwaka uliopita hakina maana leo. False assurance. HIV status inabadilika na new exposures — test ya mwaka jana haina maana leo.
3. “If you loved me, you’d trust me”“Kama ungenipenda, ungenamini”“Kama ungenipenda, ungeniamini” Guilt/trust manipulation. Love does not require risking your health. Udanganyifu wa hatia/uaminifu. Upendo hauhitaji kuhatarisha afya yako. Guilt/trust manipulation. Love haihitaji ku-risk health yako.
4. “I’ll withdraw before — it’s fine”“Nitatoka kabla — sawa tu”“Nitatoka kabla — ni fine” Dangerous myth. Withdrawal does not prevent STIs or HIV transmission. Hadithi hatari. Kutoka kabla hakuzuii magonjwa ya zinaa au maambukizi ya VVU. Dangerous myth. Withdrawal haipreventi STIs au HIV transmission.
5. “We’ve done it before without — nothing happened”“Tumefanya bila hapo awali — hakuna kilichotokea”“Tumefanya bila hapo awali — hakuna kilitokea” Normalising risk. Each unprotected exposure carries real risk regardless of past luck. Kawaida ya hatari. Kila mfiduo usio na kinga una hatari halisi bila kujali bahati ya zamani. Ku-normalise risk. Kila unprotected exposure ina real risk bila kujali bahati ya zamani.
⚠ Remember:⚠ Kumbuka:⚠ Remember: None of these are valid reasons to have unprotected sex. Your health is not negotiable. Hakuna hata moja kati ya hizi ni sababu halali ya kufanya ngono isiyo salama. Afya yako haiwezi kujadiliwa. Hakuna hata moja kati ya hizi ni valid reason ya ku-have unprotected sex. Health yako si negotiable.

Scenario: Peter, 17, has been dating a girl for 3 months. She says: "If you really loved me you would not make me wait. Everyone our age is in a physical relationship. Choosing abstinence just means you don't care about me."
What should Peter do?
She says she will end the relationship unless he agrees. What does Peter do?
✅
Values and Health Protected

Peter correctly recognises that real love never demands you abandon your values. A partner who threatens to leave unless you have sex has shown exactly why abstinence was the right choice. Abstinence is the only 100% reliable protection against HIV, STIs, and unintended pregnancy. Peter's decision protects both his health and his future.

❌
Emotional Pressure Overrode His Values

By giving in, Peter has put both himself and his partner at risk. No sexual activity is without risk of HIV, STIs, and pregnancy. A relationship founded on pressure is not a safe relationship. Speak to a trusted adult, teacher, or counsellor. NASCOP: 0800 723 253 (toll-free).

❌
Peer Pressure Won This Round

"Everyone does it" is one of the most dangerous myths among Kenyan adolescents — research shows most young people overestimate how many peers are sexually active. Someone can love their partner completely and still cause irreversible harm. Abstinence is the only path with zero risk of HIV, STIs, and pregnancy. NASCOP: 0800 723 253 (toll-free).

✅
Healthy — Seeks Guidance Together

This is a mature, healthy approach. Inviting a trusted adult into the conversation shows wisdom, not weakness. A school counsellor, teacher, or health worker can help both of them understand what a respectful, healthy relationship looks like at their age. Abstinence is the safest and most affirming choice for young people.

⚠️
Protected — But Missed a Teaching Moment

Distancing himself protects Peter but leaves the pressure unanswered. A better path: have an honest conversation: "I care about you, and that's exactly why I choose abstinence. My values are not going to change." Open communication about healthy boundaries is a sign of maturity.
